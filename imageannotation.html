Image Annotation Tools

2D Bounding Boxes

It defines objects in 2D space through graphical representations, typically used in computer vision and machine learning for classifying objects in images and videos.

3D Cuboid Annotation

This technique identifies and interprets 3D objects within images, aiding machines in understanding the depth of objects such as vehicles, people, and structures.

Landmark Annotation

Organizations leverage this method to ascertain the shape and emotion of natural objects, such as facial features and expressions, through a series of points. It aids in creating precise datasets for identifying the shapes of objects of varying sizes, allowing computers to detect smaller elements or specific target points.

Skeletal Annotation

It focuses on body movement and posture, pinpointing and labeling key skeletal features in images. It generates extensive datasets for training AI models in medical diagnostics

Polygon Annotation

It draws precise polygon contours over objects for identifying and localizing in videos and images. This technique excels at annotating complex, non-linear shapes for segmentation or detection tasks.

Semantic Segmentation

This technique segments images in computer vision by semantically segmenting an image dataset to identify all categories and classes.

3D Point Cloud Annotation

It allows for detailed object detection and classification, enabling accurate dimension measurement. 3D Point Cloud data can be matched to camera images for labeling diverse objects.

Polyline Annotation

Machine learning algorithms power autonomous vehicles by interpreting and navigating their environment through training with annotated video and image data. This technology is used in autonomous cars for detecting road lanes and adjusting movements accordingly.

Image annotation is most commonly used to identify objects, boundaries, and segments within an image. It involves classifying these objects, segments, and features with the appropriate boxes and labels.

1. Bounding Box: This method involves drawing a box around relatively symmetrical objects, such as cars, pedestrians, and road signs. It's used when object shapes are less important and occlusion is not an issue. Two-dimensional bounding boxes are used for 2D shapes, and three-dimensional bounding boxes for 3D shapes.

2. Landmarking: Used for analyzing body positioning and alignment, facial expressions, and emotions, as well as plotting data characteristics. For instance, in sports analytics, it can determine the orientation of a baseball pitcher’s elbows, hands, and wrists.

3. Masking: This technique involves image masking, which can hide certain areas of an image like a house, land area, or plant, and reveal other areas of interest, making it easier to focus on specific parts of the image.

4. Polygon: Used to mark the highest points (vertices) of images by annotating the edges of objects with irregular shapes. Annotators mark each vertex and edge of the object.

5. Polyline: Involves a continuous line made up of one or more segments plotted over a wide area. Linear structures are defined in images and videos by connecting small lines at the vertex of the shape. Annotators use annotation platforms and labeling tools to apply these lines to images.

6. Tracking: Used to label and plot an object’s movement over time in multiple video frames. Some annotation tools feature interpolation, allowing annotators to label a frame, then jump to a later frame, shifting the annotation to the new position over time.

7. Transcription: This is a word-for-word description of an audio recording. Generally, it refers to converting an audio or video record of an important conversation into editable text.
